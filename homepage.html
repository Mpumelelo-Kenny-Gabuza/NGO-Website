Survivor Story

"Finding strength in community and hope in healing - my journey with Manyana Mhlali Development transformed my life."

My name is Thandi. For a long time, I lived in a silence that was louder than any noise. After losing my job, the weight of providing for my two children felt unbearable. The stress and hopelessness were a constant cloud over me. I felt like I was failing as a mother, and the loneliness was crushing. I didn't know it then, but I was struggling with my mental health, and I had no one to talk to. At my lowest point, I heard a community health worker from Manyana Mhlali Development speaking at a local shop. She was talking about mental health, saying it was okay not to be okay, and that asking for help was a sign of strength. Those words planted a small seed of hope in me. I found the courage to attend one of MMD's support groups. Walking through that door was the hardest step I have ever taken, but it was also the first step on my new path. I was met not with judgment, but with understanding. For the first time, I shared my story and my fears. I learned that I was not alone.
